\section{Problem formulation}

Let $\Omega\subset\mathbb{R}^d$ ($d=1,2$) be a bounded domain with sufficiently smooth boundary $\partial\Omega$, let $T>0$, and set $Q:=\Omega\times(0,T)$ and $\Sigma:=\partial\Omega\times(0,T)$. We seek a state $y:Q\to\mathbb{R}$ and a Dirichlet boundary control $u:\Sigma\to\mathbb{R}$ minimizing
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:cost}
J(y,u)=\frac12\int_Q |y-y_d|^2\,dx\,dt
       +\frac{\alpha}{2}\int_\Sigma |u|^2\,ds\,dt,
\end{equation}
subject to
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:state}
\begin{cases}
\partial_t y-\nu\Delta y+f(y)=g, & \text{in }Q,\\
y=u, & \text{on }\Sigma,\\
y(x,0)=y_0(x), & \text{in }\Omega.
\end{cases}
\end{equation}
Here $\nu>0$ is the diffusion coefficient, $g$ is a prescribed source term, $y_d\in L^2(Q)$ is the desired trajectory, and $\alpha>0$ is the Tikhonov regularization parameter. The source $g$ is fixed problem data and is not an optimization variable. The case $g=0$ is included. The boundary condition is of Dirichlet type, so the control acts directly on the boundary trace of the state.
